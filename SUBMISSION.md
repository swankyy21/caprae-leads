## Business Understanding

### What is Caprae's Mission?

Caprae Capital's mission is to fundamentally change how value is created in private equity — not through financial engineering, but through deliberate operational transformation. At the heart of their model is a conviction that the greatest opportunity in any acquisition is not the deal itself, but the seven-year journey that follows it. By treating M&A as a long-term operating endeavor rather than a transactional event, Caprae aims to turn structurally sound businesses into category leaders.

Central to this mission is AI. Caprae believes that artificial intelligence is the most powerful lever available to small and mid-sized businesses today — not as a buzzword, but as a practical toolkit that can streamline operations, sharpen decision-making, and unlock growth that would otherwise take years of headcount to achieve. Through their SaaS and MaaS (M&A as a Service) models, Caprae brings this infrastructure directly into the companies they acquire and support, embedding intelligent systems that compound over time.

What distinguishes Caprae's mission from traditional PE is the emphasis on partnership over extraction. They are not optimizing for a quick multiple — they are building businesses that are structurally better, operationally leaner, and genuinely more valuable because of the systems put in place post-acquisition. That long-term, builder-first orientation is rare in private equity, and it is precisely what makes Caprae's mission meaningful rather than just strategic.

In short, Caprae exists to prove that the best investment a PE firm can make is not in the deal structure, but in the people, processes, and technology that come after the ink dries.

### How is Caprae Changing the ETA Space and Broader PE?

Entrepreneurship Through Acquisition has historically been an under-resourced path. Search fund operators typically acquire a business, inherit its existing systems and staff, and spend years learning through trial and error how to run and grow it. The support infrastructure — operational playbooks, technology tools, strategic guidance — has largely been absent or inconsistent. Caprae changes this by bringing a structured, technology-first operating model into the ETA equation. Rather than leaving operators to figure it out alone, Caprae arrives with AI tools, SaaS infrastructure, and a repeatable framework for post-acquisition transformation. This materially lowers the risk of the ETA model and raises the ceiling on what an operator can achieve.

In the broader PE landscape, Caprae represents a genuine philosophical shift. Traditional private equity generates returns through a combination of multiple expansion, leverage, and cost cutting. These levers are finite — and in an environment of higher interest rates and compressed multiples, the firms that will outperform are those that can create operational value, not just financial value. Caprae's thesis is that AI-driven operational improvement is the new alpha in PE, and they are building the infrastructure to systematically deliver it across a portfolio.

This is significant not just as a strategy, but as a model for the industry. By treating AI as a core competency rather than an add-on, Caprae is creating a template that other PE firms will eventually be forced to follow. The difference is that Caprae is building this capability now, from the ground up, with a team that understands both the technology and the operational context. That first-mover advantage — in talent, tooling, and institutional knowledge — is what will differentiate Caprae's portfolio companies from those backed by firms still relying on spreadsheets and gut instinct.
